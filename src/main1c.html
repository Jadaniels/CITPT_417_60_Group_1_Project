Young Reader's Review

Cinder: Book One of the Lunar Chronicles

Humans and androids crowd the raucous streets of New Beijing. A deadly plague ravages the population. From space, a ruthless lunar people watch, waiting to make their move. No one knows that Earth's fate hinges on one girl. . . . Cinder, a gifted mechanic, is a cyborg. She's a second-class citizen with a mysterious past, reviled by her stepmother and blamed for her stepsister's illness. But when her life becomes intertwined with the handsome Prince Kai's, she suddenly finds herself at the center of an intergalactic struggle, and a forbidden attraction. Caught between duty and freedom, loyalty and betrayal, she must uncover secrets about her past in order to protect her world's future. With high-stakes action and a smart, resourceful heroine, Cinder is a Cinderella retelling that is at once classic and strikingly original.

-Amazon

Easy to read, imaginative, and sets the stage for the follow on books that integrate stories that you are familiar, but did not know share the same universe.

Cinder is the first of a series of books by Marissa Meyer. It is a different take of the popular Cinderella story. The story is set in an alternative universe similar to ours. Magic and technology is present. Magic being used by Lunar people while Earth people rely on technology. The world as post-apocalyptic with a large divide between the "haves" and "haves not". Cinder is an enduring tomboy that captures your heart. She is faced with a horrible family life with her younger sister and artificial intelligent robot being her only comfort. In the beginning of the story, she is enamored by a young man and throughout the story the plot is expanded across the globe. Her involvement exposes political secrets, government conspiracies, and an adventure that keeps you wanting to read more.

The Looking Glass Wars

The Myth: Alice was an ordinary girl who stepped through the looking glass and entered a fairy-tale world invented by Lewis Carroll in his famous storybook. The Truth: Wonderland is real. Alyss Heart is the heir to the throne, until her murderous aunt Redd steals the crown and kills Alyss? parents. To escape Redd, Alyss and her bodyguard, Hatter Madigan, must flee to our world through the Pool of Tears. But in the pool Alyss and Hatter are separated. Lost and alone in Victorian London, Alyss is befriended by an aspiring author to whom she tells the violent, heartbreaking story of her young life. Yet he gets the story all wrong. Hatter Madigan knows the truth only too well, and he is searching every corner of our world to find the lost princess and return her to Wonderland so she may battle Redd for her rightful place as the Queen of Hearts.

-Amazon

A wonderful retelling of what happens when an author takes creative licensing to a story. The story entices you to read more and follow on to the remaining of the series where maybe Queen Redd is not as bad of a person you thought she was.

I was very intrigued by The Looking Glass Wars. The cover and artwork is reminisce to Star Wars. Alyss is heir to the throne and escapes the castle through the Pool of Tears into London when her aunt lays siege. She is alone and is taking in by a family and her story is changed to a fairy tale for all to read. While those around her brainwash her that it is all a dream, her comrades' fight a rebellion for her kingdom. Her retainer, Hatter Madigan searches the world to find Alyss after being separated in transit. Dodge, the son of the original captain of the palace guards along with General Doppelganger create the Alyssian rebellion. They struggle for thirteen years battling against Queen Redd and The Cat until Alyss and Madigan return to take the throne back. Deception, loss, and finally resolution are gained, as Alyss becomes the "Warrior-Queen" to rule Wonderland.
