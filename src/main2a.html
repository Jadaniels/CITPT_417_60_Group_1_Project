Last Week's Reviews

Lies We Tell Ourselves

This novel is set in 1959 Davisburg, Virginia. The story follows Sarah who is part of the first group of African American students to attend jefferson Highschool. The integration is hard and Sarah experiences rejection and abuse from her peers. The situation becomes even more difficult when Linda, a white friend, and her fall for each other.
The story is great read from start to finish while painting a picture of the individual struggles faced by African Americans during the transition away from segregation.
Score: 4.5/5

Salt To The Sea

The novel takes place in 1945 Prussia. It follows the stroy of a group of young refugees as they run from the war, facing horror after horror along the way. You slowly learn more about the different group members, like their nationality, personality, and individual struggles they are trying to escape from including the war. It's a good stroy, if a bit slow paced.
Score: 3.8/5
