Wicked: The Life and Times of the Wicked Witch of the West (Wicked Years)

When Dorothy triumphed over the Wicked Witch of the West in L. Frank Baum's classic tale, we heard only her side of the story. But what about her arch-nemesis, the mysterious witch? Where did she come from? How did she become so wicked? And what is the true nature of evil? Gregory Maguire creates a fantasy world so rich and vivid that we will never look at Oz the same way again. Wicked is about a land where animals talk and strive to be treated like first-class citizens, Munchkinlanders seek the comfort of middle-class stability and the Tin Man becomes a victim of domestic violence. And then there is the little green-skinned girl named Elphaba, who will grow up to be the infamous Wicked Witch of the West, a smart, prickly and misunderstood creature who challenges all our preconceived notions about the nature of good and evil.

-Amazon

A wonderful retelling of what happens when an author takes creative licensing to a story. The story entices you to read more and follow on to the remaining of the series where maybe Queen Redd is not as bad of a person you thought she was.

I was very intrigued by The Looking Glass Wars. The cover and artwork is reminisce to Star Wars. Alyss is heir to the throne and escapes the castle through the Pool of Tears into London when her aunt lays siege. She is alone and is taking in by a family and her story is changed to a fairy tale for all to read. While those around her brainwash her that it is all a dream, her comrades’ fight a rebellion for her kingdom. Her retainer, Hatter Madigan searches the world to find Alyss after being separated in transit. Dodge, the son of the original captain of the palace guards along with General Doppelgänger create the Alyssian rebellion. They struggle for thirteen years battling against Queen Redd and The Cat until Alyss and Madigan return to take the throne back. Deception, loss, and finally resolution are gained, as Alyss becomes the “Warrior-Queen” to rule Wonderland.

Dead Witch Walking

All the creatures of the night gather in "the Hollows" of Cincinnati, to hide, to prowl, to party . . . and to feed. Vampires rule the darkness in a predator-eat-predator world rife with dangers beyond imagining—and it's Rachel Morgan's job to keep that world civilized. A bounty hunter and a witch with serious sex appeal and an attitude, she'll bring 'em back alive, dead . . . or undead.

-Amazon

Thrilling rollercoaster of activity. Recommended for adult readers due to sexual content and graphic imagery. Many books in the series that will keep you entertained.

Dead Witch Walking drew me in due to the all titles of the series being takes from movie titles. This one being Dead Man Walking. Some of the other book titles are The Good, The Bad, and The Undead and Every Which Way But Dead. Dead Witch Walking introduces us to Rachel Morgan. A stubborn, sexy red-hair witch that works for law enforcement. She wants out of the corrupted police department. With the help of Eve, a blonde, frigid vampire and Jenks, a four-inch pixie they venture out to be bounty hunters. Along the way to earning freedom from buying their way out of the police force, they take on Trent, a possible drug dealer posing as prominent businessperson. All the while, Rachel and Eve are conflicted as they share more than blood.
