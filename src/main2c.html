Wicked: The Life and Times of the Wicked Witch of the West (Wicked Years)

When Dorothy triumphed over the Wicked Witch of the West in L. Frank Baum's classic tale, we heard only her side of the story. But what about her arch-nemesis, the mysterious witch? Where did she come from? How did she become so wicked? And what is the true nature of evil? Gregory Maguire creates a fantasy world so rich and vivid that we will never look at Oz the same way again. Wicked is about a land where animals talk and strive to be treated like first-class citizens, Munchkinlanders seek the comfort of middle-class stability and the Tin Man comes a victim of domestic violence. And then there is the little green-skinned girl named Elphaba, who will grow up to be the infamous Wicked Witch of the West, a smart, prickly and misunderstood creature who challenges all our preconceived notions about the nature of good and evil.

-Amazon

Great read, but recommended for more advanced readers due to the graphic content and concepts. Heart wrecking at times and makes you root for the underdog.

Wicked is one of several alternate stories by Gregory Maguire. The overall story is similar to Wicked Witch of the West, but from the viewpoint of the Wicked Witch, Elphaba. Wicked is a wonderful book illustrating the drastic difference between "beautiful" people and others. While the original story highlights the harsh treatment to Dorothy and her travels to get home. Wicked shows mistreatment, bulling, and loneliness that Elphaba faced by her family and those around her. Her mother who took shady medicine prior to giving birth afflicted Elphaba with deformity. Her isolation drove her to create her only friends, the flying monkeys that Dorothy and party promptly hurt and kill. She is deprived of her birthright, the ruby slippers that are emotionally connected to her sister Nessarose. Elphaba is shown to be a highly intelligent person striving for equal rights for the people. She is harassed and targeted by those around her due to her looks and beliefs.

Thrilling rollercoaster of activity. Recommended for adult readers due to sexual content and graphic imagery. Many books in the series that will keep you entertained.

Dead Witch Walking drew me in due to the all titles of the series being takes from movie titles. This one being Dead Man Walking. Some of the other book titles are The Good, The Bad, and The Undead and Every Which Way But Dead. Dead Witch Walking introduces us to Rachel Morgan. A stubborn, sexy red-hair witch that works for law enforcement. She wants out of the corrupted police department. With the help of Eve, a blonde, frigid vampire and Jenks, a four-inch pixie they venture out to be bounty hunters. Along the way to earning freedom from buying their way out of the police force, they take on Trent, a possible drug dealer posing as prominent businessperson. All the while, Rachel and Eve are conflicted as they share more than blood.
